## Overview

Each player brings a single preconstructed deck into a match.

Every deck is associated with a Wizard. The selected Wizard determines which Schools of Magic may be included in the deck and provides the player's starting preconstructed deck.

Players may freely modify a Wizard's starting deck provided the resulting deck remains legal.

---

# Wizard Selection

Before a match begins, each player selects a Wizard.

A Wizard determines:

- The Schools of Magic permitted within the deck.
    
- The starter deck unlocked with that Wizard.
    
- The Wizard represented during gameplay.
    

Unless explicitly stated otherwise, Wizards do not provide gameplay abilities or passive effects during a match.

---

# School Restrictions

Each Wizard defines one or more permitted Schools of Magic.

A deck may only contain cards belonging to the Schools permitted by its selected Wizard.

Examples include:

- Arcane
    
- Pyromancy
    
- Divine
    
- Arcane / Divine
    
- Nature / Necromancy
    

The permitted School combinations are defined by each individual Wizard.

---

# Starter Decks

Every Wizard unlocks a preconstructed starter deck.

Starter decks are intended to introduce players to that Wizard's preferred playstyle and Schools of Magic.

Players may modify a starter deck at any time, provided the resulting deck remains legal.

---

# Deck Size

A deck must contain exactly the required number of cards.

Deck size is a configurable gameplay constant.

All players must use the same deck size unless modified by the selected game mode.

---

# Card Copies

A deck may contain multiple copies of the same card.

The maximum number of copies permitted for a card is a configurable gameplay constant.

Individual cards may define additional deckbuilding restrictions.

---

# Legendary Cards

Legendary cards represent unique individuals, artifacts, or magical entities.

Unless explicitly stated otherwise, a deck may contain only one copy of a Legendary card.

---

# Card Legality

Only legal cards may be included in a deck.

Card legality is determined by the selected game format.

Game formats may define:

- Allowed card sets
    
- Restricted cards
    
- Banned cards
    
- Rotation rules
    

---

# Sideboards

Standard constructed play does not use a sideboard.

Future game modes may introduce sideboards with their own construction rules.

---

# Match Initialization

At the beginning of a match:

1. The player's deck is validated.
    
2. The deck is shuffled.
    
3. The deck is placed face down.
    
4. Cards are drawn according to the match rules.
    

The order of cards within a deck is hidden information.

---

# Deck Validation

A deck is considered legal only if it satisfies all applicable deck construction rules.

An illegal deck cannot be used to begin a match.

Deck validation should occur before matchmaking or gameplay begins.

---

# Future Expansion

Future mechanics may extend the deck construction system with additional formats or restrictions.

Examples include:

- Draft
    
- Sealed
    
- Singleton
    
- Campaign-specific deck rules
    
- Wizard-specific challenges
    

These mechanics should extend the existing deck construction rules rather than replace them.

---

# Design Principles

The deck construction system should:

- Encourage strategic planning.
    
- Support multiple viable archetypes.
    
- Reward experimentation.
    
- Promote replayability.
    
- Preserve meaningful deckbuilding choices.
    

Wizard selection should define a player's magical identity without introducing additional gameplay complexity during a match.
