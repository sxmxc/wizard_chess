## Overview

Wizards represent the player within the world of Wizard Chess.

Every deck is associated with a Wizard. Wizards define a player's magical identity, determine which Schools of Magic may be included in a deck, and provide a starting preconstructed deck.

Wizards are intentionally designed to have minimal impact on gameplay during a match. The focus of Wizard Chess is the interaction between chess and cards, not hero abilities.

---

# Wizard Responsibilities

Every Wizard defines the following:

- Name
    
- Portrait
    
- Character model
    
- Voice and audio
    
- Intro and victory animations
    
- Permitted Schools of Magic
    
- Starter deck
    
- Lore
    

Additional cosmetic customization may be introduced in future updates.

---

# School Affinity

Each Wizard is associated with one or more Schools of Magic.

These Schools determine which cards may be included in decks built for that Wizard.

The number of permitted Schools is defined individually for each Wizard.

Examples include:

- Arcane
    
- Pyromancy
    
- Nature
    
- Divine
    
- Necromancy
    
- Arcane / Divine
    
- Nature / Pyromancy
    

---

# Starter Deck

Each Wizard unlocks a preconstructed starter deck.

Starter decks are intended to introduce the Wizard's preferred playstyle and available Schools.

Players may freely modify a starter deck provided the resulting deck remains legal.

---

# Gameplay

Unless explicitly modified by a game mode, Wizards do not provide:

- Passive abilities
    
- Activated abilities
    
- Hero powers
    
- Resource generation
    
- Victory conditions
    
- Special rules
    

Gameplay is determined entirely by the chessboard, the player's deck, and the rules of the game.

---

# Customization

Players may customize the appearance of their Wizard without affecting gameplay.

Examples include:

- Alternate outfits
    
- Portraits
    
- Titles
    
- Emotes
    
- Board entrance animations
    
- Victory animations
    

Cosmetic customization should never provide a gameplay advantage.

---

# Unlocking

Wizards may be unlocked through:

- Progression
    
- Campaign completion
    
- Challenges
    
- Achievements
    
- Future content updates
    

Unlocking a Wizard also unlocks that Wizard's starter deck.

---

# AI Wizards

Computer-controlled opponents also use Wizards.

AI Wizards follow the same deck construction rules as human players.

The selected Wizard determines the Schools of Magic available to the AI's deck.

AI opponents receive no gameplay advantages from their Wizard.

---

# Future Expansion

Future Wizards may introduce:

- New School combinations
    
- New starter decks
    
- Additional cosmetic options
    
- Campaign-specific characters
    

Future Wizards should not introduce new gameplay systems that alter the core rules of Wizard Chess.

---

# Design Principles

Wizards should:

- Define a player's magical identity.
    
- Encourage different deckbuilding strategies.
    
- Introduce players to different Schools of Magic.
    
- Serve as progression milestones.
    
- Enhance the game's presentation and personality.
    

The strategic depth of Wizard Chess should always come from the interaction between chess and cards rather than from Wizard-specific gameplay abilities.
