## Overview

Wizard Chess is played as a series of alternating turns.

Each turn consists of five phases that are resolved in order.

Unless modified by card text, phases may not be skipped.

The active player completes all phases before play passes to the opposing player.

---

## Turn Order

Each turn consists of the following phases:

1. Beginning Phase
    
2. Preparation Phase
    
3. Move Phase
    
4. Reaction Phase
    
5. End Phase
    

Once the End Phase has completed, the turn immediately passes to the opposing player.

---

# Beginning Phase

The Beginning Phase prepares the active player for their turn.

The following actions occur in order:

1. Resolve all **Beginning of Turn** effects.
    
2. Increase Maximum Mana by the configured amount.
    
3. Refresh available Mana.
    
4. Draw one card.
    
5. Generate and resolve any resulting game events.
    

After all generated game events have been resolved, the Beginning Phase ends.

---

# Preparation Phase

The Preparation Phase allows the active player to prepare for their mandatory chess move.

During this phase, the active player may perform any number of legal actions, including:

- Play Unit cards.
    
- Cast Spell cards.
    
- Play Artifact cards.
    
- Play Environment cards.
    
- Set Trap cards.
    
- Activate abilities that are legal during the Preparation Phase.
    

Cards played during this phase become active immediately unless their card text states otherwise.

Playing cards or activating abilities may generate one or more game events.

All generated game events are resolved before additional actions may be performed.

The active player may continue performing legal actions until they choose to proceed to the Move Phase.

Once the Move Phase begins, the Preparation Phase cannot be revisited.

---

# Move Phase

The Move Phase is the core gameplay phase.

During this phase, the active player must perform exactly one legal chess move unless modified by card text.

The moved chess piece uses all currently active abilities, movement modifications, keywords, and effects.

Completing the chess move may generate one or more game events.

All generated game events are resolved before the Move Phase ends.

After all generated game events have been resolved, the Move Phase immediately proceeds to the Reaction Phase.

---

# Reaction Phase

The Reaction Phase allows players to respond to game events generated during the current turn.

Reaction cards and abilities may only be played during this phase unless explicitly stated otherwise.

Playing reactions or activating abilities may generate additional game events.

The order in which reactions are played and game events are resolved is defined in **Section 6 - Effect Resolution**.

When both players consecutively decline to perform additional reactions and all generated game events have been resolved, the Reaction Phase ends.

---

# End Phase

The End Phase concludes the current turn.

The following actions occur in order:

1. Resolve all **End of Turn** effects.
    
2. Remove temporary effects that expire at the end of the turn.
    
3. Enforce the maximum hand size.
    
4. Generate and resolve any resulting game events.
    
5. End the current turn.
    

After all generated game events have been resolved, the turn ends and play passes to the opposing player.

---

# Phase Restrictions

Each phase defines which actions are legal.

Unless explicitly permitted by card text or game rules, actions may only be performed during their appropriate phase.

Examples include:

- Unit cards may normally only be played during the Preparation Phase.
    
- Reaction cards may normally only be played during the Reaction Phase.
    
- Chess movement occurs only during the Move Phase.
    

Cards may explicitly override these restrictions.

---

# Mandatory Movement

Every player turn must include exactly one legal chess move.

A player may not voluntarily end their turn before completing the Move Phase.

If no legal move exists, the game proceeds according to the standard rules of chess.

---

# Priority

Only one player has priority at any given time.

Outside of the Reaction Phase, the active player has priority.

Priority during the Reaction Phase is defined in **Section 6 - Effect Resolution**.

---

# Turn Summary

A standard turn proceeds as follows:

1. Beginning Phase
    
    - Resolve beginning of turn effects.
        
    - Increase Maximum Mana.
        
    - Refresh Mana.
        
    - Draw one card.
        
    - Resolve generated game events.
        
2. Preparation Phase
    
    - Play cards.
        
    - Activate abilities.
        
    - Resolve generated game events.
        
    - Continue until the player proceeds to the Move Phase.
        
3. Move Phase
    
    - Perform one mandatory legal chess move.
        
    - Resolve generated game events.
        
4. Reaction Phase
    
    - Play reactions.
        
    - Activate reaction abilities.
        
    - Resolve generated game events.
        
    - Continue until both players pass consecutively.
        
5. End Phase
    
    - Resolve end of turn effects.
        
    - Remove expiring effects.
        
    - Enforce maximum hand size.
        
    - Resolve generated game events.
        
    - End the turn.
