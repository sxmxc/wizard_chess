## Overview

Every card in Wizard Chess belongs to a School of Magic.

A card's School represents the magical discipline from which it originates and defines its overall gameplay philosophy.

Schools are used for:

- Card identity
    
- Deckbuilding
    
- Card synergies
    
- Future expansion mechanics
    

A card belongs to exactly one School unless explicitly stated otherwise.

---

# Arcane

Arcane magic focuses on knowledge, manipulation, and control.

Arcane cards excel at repositioning pieces, manipulating movement, drawing cards, and altering the flow of the game.

### Primary Themes

- Movement manipulation
    
- Teleportation
    
- Card draw
    
- Board control
    
- Spell interaction
    
- Tactical flexibility
    

### Typical Mechanics

- Modify movement rules
    
- Move friendly or enemy pieces
    
- Copy effects
    
- Return cards to hand
    
- Rearrange the battlefield
    

---

# Pyromancy

Pyromancy focuses on destruction and battlefield pressure.

Pyromancy cards reward aggressive play and forcing difficult positional decisions.

### Primary Themes

- Direct destruction
    
- Area denial
    
- Burning
    
- Offensive pressure
    
- Risk vs reward
    

### Typical Mechanics

- Destroy pieces
    
- Damage areas of the board
    
- Burning effects
    
- Force movement
    
- Punish clustered pieces
    

---

# Nature

Nature magic focuses on growth, adaptation, and resilience.

Nature cards strengthen pieces over time and reward long-term planning.

### Primary Themes

- Growth
    
- Healing
    
- Adaptation
    
- Development
    
- Sustainability
    

### Typical Mechanics

- Strengthen Units
    
- Remove negative effects
    
- Improve positioning
    
- Enhance Pawns
    
- Resource generation
    

---

# Divine

Divine magic focuses on protection, order, and discipline.

Divine cards reward careful positioning and defensive play.

### Primary Themes

- Protection
    
- Blessings
    
- Purification
    
- Defensive tactics
    
- Board stability
    

### Typical Mechanics

- Prevent captures
    
- Remove curses
    
- Grant protective keywords
    
- Support nearby allies
    
- Counter hostile effects
    

---

# Necromancy

Necromancy manipulates death and sacrifice.

Necromancy rewards players willing to exchange material for long-term advantage.

### Primary Themes

- Sacrifice
    
- Resurrection
    
- Curses
    
- Attrition
    
- Graveyard interaction
    

### Typical Mechanics

- Revive captured pieces
    
- Interact with the Graveyard
    
- Apply negative effects
    
- Reward captured pieces
    
- Exchange pieces for powerful effects
    

---

# School Identity

Each School should have a clearly identifiable gameplay style.

Players should be able to recognize a card's School based on its mechanics.

Whenever possible, mechanics should reinforce the identity of their School.

---

# Multi-School Mechanics

Cards normally belong to a single School.

Future expansions may introduce mechanics that reference or interact with multiple Schools.

Cards should not belong to multiple Schools unless a future mechanic explicitly requires it.

---

# School Balance

No School should be considered inherently stronger than another.

Each School should offer unique strategic strengths and weaknesses.

Every School should support multiple viable deck archetypes.

---

# Design Principles

When designing cards for a School:

- Reinforce the School's identity.
    
- Avoid duplicating another School's primary mechanics.
    
- Introduce new strategic decisions rather than raw power.
    
- Encourage interaction with the chessboard.
    
- Reward positioning and planning.
    

The distinction between Schools should remain clear even as future expansions introduce new mechanics.
