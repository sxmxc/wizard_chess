## Overview

Keywords are standardized gameplay mechanics used to represent rules that appear frequently across multiple cards.

Keywords improve readability, reduce repetitive rules text, and provide a consistent vocabulary for players.

A keyword has no gameplay meaning unless it is explicitly defined within this section.

New keywords should only be introduced when a mechanic is expected to appear across a significant number of cards.

---

# Keyword Types

Wizard Chess currently recognizes three categories of keywords.

## Ability Keywords

Ability Keywords grant a persistent gameplay mechanic.

Examples include:

- Ward
    

---

## Status Keywords

Status Keywords represent temporary conditions affecting a chess piece.

Status Keywords may be applied and removed by card effects.

Examples include:

- Burning
    

---

## Trait Keywords

Trait Keywords identify a piece as belonging to a particular category for the purposes of other card effects.

Trait Keywords have no inherent gameplay effect unless referenced by another card.

Examples include:

- Human
    
- Undead
    
- Royal
    

Additional Trait Keywords may be introduced in future expansions.

---

# Core Keywords

## Ward

Prevent the next capture of this piece.

After preventing a capture, Ward is removed.

A piece cannot have more than one instance of Ward.

---

## Burning

At the end of its controller's next End Phase, this piece is captured.

Burning is then removed.

If a piece is already Burning, applying Burning again has no additional effect unless explicitly stated otherwise.

---

# Keyword Stacking

Unless explicitly stated otherwise:

- A keyword cannot exist multiple times on the same piece.
    
- Applying an existing keyword again has no additional effect.
    
- Removing a keyword completely removes its effects.
    

Cards may explicitly override these rules.

---

# Keyword Duration

A keyword remains active until one of the following occurs:

- Its duration expires.
    
- It is removed by another effect.
    
- The affected piece leaves the board.
    
- The source of the keyword specifies otherwise.
    

Card text always determines a keyword's duration.

---

# Keyword Interactions

A chess piece may have multiple different keywords simultaneously.

Each keyword functions independently unless another card explicitly modifies that interaction.

---

# Future Keywords

New keywords should only be introduced when they simplify mechanics that are repeated across many cards.

Mechanics that appear infrequently should be written directly on the card instead of becoming keywords.

---

# Design Principles

Keywords should adhere to the following principles:

- Represent a single, clearly defined mechanic.
    
- Be deterministic and unambiguous.
    
- Be reusable across many cards.
    
- Be concise enough to replace repeated rules text.
    
- Enhance readability without increasing complexity.
    

Keywords should simplify the game, not complicate it.
